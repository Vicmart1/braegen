Background
Glioblastoma Multiforme, or GBM, is the most common and deadly primary brain cancer. 1 in 5 of all brain tumors are GBM, and over 18,000 new patients in the US are diagnosed with GBM every year, including loved ones and familiar faces like John McCain. The median overall survival for a GBM patient is just over 14 months.


In other words, diagnosis is essentially a death sentence.


So what treatment options are there?


The Stupp Protocol is the standard-of-care treatment of surgery followed by adjuct radiotherapy and chemotherapy. However, GBM's unique characteristics negates the effects of treatment. The infiltrative growth of GBM makes total surgical resection impossible and radiation therapy difficult without damaging healthy tissue. But perhaps the largest rate limiting obstacle for treating primary brain tumors in patients is the blood-brain barrier. The BBB prevents 99.9% of drugs from reaching tumor sites, rendering oral or intravenous chemotherapy ineffective. And past six months, there is no standard-of-care.


Zero.


Zero effective treatment for treating one of the most deadly brain cancers.


In order to change that, we realized we need to disrupt the industry with something revolutionary.


And we have the solution - an implantable device that enables direct drug delivery. Being direct bypasses the blood brain barrier, and being long-term fights against recurrence over time. With all this tech, we're still not compromising other key criteria for GBM treatment.


Because our goal isn't just to innovate cancer treatment, it's to disrupt.

Since then, the team has grown dramatically, both in terms of students and partners.
