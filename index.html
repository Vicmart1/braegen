Background
Brain tumors represent an incredibly deadly type of cancer, with over 75,000 new cases per year. Paired with quickly worsening symptoms and shockingly limited treatment options, prognosis is often grim for recently diagnosed patients.


Take Glioblastoma Multiforme, or GBM, for example - it is the most common and deadly primary brain cancer. 1 in 5 of all brain tumors are GBM, and over 18,000 new patients in the US are diagnosed with GBM every year - often times loved ones and familiar faces. The median overall survival for a GBM patient is just over 14 months.

So what treatment options are there?


The Stupp Protocol is the standard-of-care treatment of surgery followed by adjuvant radiotherapy and chemotherapy. However, GBM's unique characteristics negates the effects of treatment. The infiltrative growth of GBM makes total surgical resection impossible and radiation therapy difficult without damaging healthy tissue. But perhaps the largest rate limiting obstacle for treating primary brain tumors in patients is the blood-brain barrier. The BBB prevents 99.9% of drugs from reaching tumor sites, rendering oral or intravenous chemotherapy ineffective.
Figure 2: A computer generated representation of the dense Blood Brain Barrier.
Past six months, there is no standard-of-care.


Zero.


Zero effective treatment for treating one of the most deadly brain cancers.


In order to change that, and help treat all types of brain tumors, we realized we need to disrupt the industry with something entirely new. And we have the solution - an patent-pending implantable device that enables direct drug delivery. Being direct bypasses the blood brain barrier, and being long-term fights against recurrence over time.


Unlike the competition, we're not compromising any key criteria for brain tumor treatment.


Because our goal isn't just to innovate brain cancer treatment, it's to revolutionize it.
